Combustion Of Organic Molecules
Glucose And ATP

The most common energy source are sugars or more specifically glucose. These sugars that we eat must be broken down in order for us to get the energy from them. The breaking down is called combustion and the formula is:

C6H12O6(s) + 6O2(g) --> 6CO2(g) + 6H2O(g)

Glycolysis And Thermochemistry
Thermochemical Applications In Glycolysis

Glucose goes through a 10 step process called glycolysis, the steps have exothermic or endothermic properties when glycolysis is taking place. And it is very important because this process helps derive energy from glucose.

Sweating Is Exothermic

When humans workout and do physical activities, their body temperatures rise, and to cool off, the body releases sweat and this process is exothermic

How Do Humans Sweat?

Exothermic reactions take place when heat flows out of a system into its surroundings and the human body is an open system that exchanges matter and energy.
